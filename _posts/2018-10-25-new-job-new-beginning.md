I wanted to have this blog up-to-date, and keep on a consistent routine of reading, experimenting and posting here. However, my internship at the National Institute of Informatics of Japan (and Tokyo's nightlife, not only is work to blame) kept me away from my goal. Moreover, once my time there finished, I spent some crazy time in Seoul, before coming back to my hometown with my hands empty - no job and with my Master thesis on the final stage, so barely anything to do.

For some months I was surveying the Japanese job market in AI and Deep Learning, and although I must say the degree at which Japan and Japanese companies have placed themselves when it comes to techs is absolutely awesome, a part of me was still reluctant to join a company. They all offered very nice packages, relocation, exciting career opportunities, and even the smallest of the companies I interviewed with was indeed eager to run for the latest Deep Learning techniques or submitting their research to academic journals and conferences. Even though salaries are not as high as they might be in the US or other European countries, I do think if you like Japanese culture and lifestyle you can make a great deal by joining a Japanese start-up company. Sincerely, if you're young, with no commitments at sight, and want to live a crazyness of an adventure, you won't regret it.

That's supernice, a sweet story is always welcome, but as every story ever told, it must have an end. After last summer, I had a solid job offer in Tokyo I was eager to accept. It turns out that on my way to the embassy (actually going down my place's stairs), I got a phone call from a professor I knew from the research group I had worked in before going to the NII, saying there was an opening for a PhD position if I wanted to join them.

So, that's how I am enrolling a PhD programme at the Technical University of Madrid! By the time of writing this a couple of weeks have passed by, and I couldn't be happier. I promise this time I will keep this blog updated with my latest research, providing learning materials from both my own work and other's, paper reviews, experiments I think of... Will I be able to catch up with you more often? 

P.S: I don't want to finish this piece of writing without mentioning the Deparment of Electronic Engineering (Technical University of Madrid). I also want to send a particularly warm and huge thank you to both my research group, the **Speech Technology Group** (Grupo de Tecnología del Habla), and my Thesis supervisor, **Prof. Fernando Fernández Martínez**, who I am not still sure knows who's he taking in, for this great oppotunity they're giving me. I only hope I can live up to the expectations.
